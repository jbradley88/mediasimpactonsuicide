How the Media Negatively Impacts Suicide

In recent years, studies have been conducted to see if there was a correlation between social media use and suicide rates. The results of these studies concluded that social media's poses an accessible outlet for people to be cyberbullied, red how-to articles on suicide, obtain items to harm themselves, watch videos that justify their feelings, an have their feelings of isolation magnified.

Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is the harassment and/or bullying of another person through any online resource. Cyberbullying has grown increasingly more common among the American population, mainly teens, as the amount of the social media outlets has increased. Cyberbullying has had a direct impact on suicide rates because it remove the geographical barriers of bullying. Of a student is bullied in school, they can usually be safe within their own home. However, social media and cyberbullying has allowed predators to bully people even when they're not physically together. A study was conducted on middle school students on how social media affects their life at home. About 2000 students said they have been victims of some sort of cyberbullying and are two times more likely to attempt suicide.

Online Resources

Unlike ever before, the internet gives people the ability to find information on a topic they wish to research with a simple click of a button. This is usually beneficial to the society when someone needs to find an Apple Pie recipe, but it less beneficial when someone is searching instructions on how to harm themselves. When someone is having suicidal thoughts and contemplating the idea of ending their own life, the internet can provide articles explaining various processes of how to commit suicide. In addition, a person can easily search youtube or other video platform websites to find videos of people harming themselves. With this information readily available, a person might feel that their suicidal feelings are justified and push them over the edge.

Extreme Groups

When someone is feeling down about themselves and do not have options to gain support, they may reach out to online sources in an attempt to feel needed. While many online sources are aimed at helping individuals who are in need, there are also organizations who are aimed to take advantage of vulnerable individuals. When an organization is able to manipulate an individual, they may be convinced to commit actions that what not normally do.
